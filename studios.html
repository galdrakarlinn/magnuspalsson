Studios & Workspaces

Throughout his career spanning over six decades, Magnús Pálsson worked in various studio spaces across Reykjavík and internationally. These workspaces were integral to his creative process, from the early conceptual works and plaster sculptures to later sound poetry and performance pieces.

This chronological documentation shows the evolution of his working environments, each reflecting different phases of his artistic development and the practical needs of his diverse practice encompassing sculpture, performance, sound art, theater, and teaching.

Hvarf - Garage Studio

The garage at Hvarf was converted into studio space from 1962, providing practical workspace for larger projects and material storage. These rare black and white photographs document the actual working garage before it was destroyed by fire in 1969, showing the rustic but functional space where important artworks were created. This studio was active during the entire Bestu stykkin period (1965-1967) and where the controversial cloth figure series was actually created, making it the birthplace of Magnús Pálsson's most provocative early work.

Tags: rural studio converted garage bestu stykkin period destroyed by fire 1969 1962-1969

Period: 1962 - 1969 (destroyed by fire)

Studio Practice & Creative Process

Magnús Pálsson's choice of studio spaces reflected his evolving artistic needs:

  • Plaster work period - Spaces with good ventilation and concrete floors for casting
  • Sound poetry development - Quiet areas for recording and acoustic experimentation
  • Teaching collaborations - Accessible spaces for student workshops and group projects
  • Archive maintenance - Organized storage systems for preserving decades of work

The chronological documentation shows how each workspace was adapted to support his multidisciplinary practice, from traditional sculpture to experimental performance and conceptual art projects, spanning over six decades of creative evolution.

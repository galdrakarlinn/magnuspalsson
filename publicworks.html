2014

  • Sólur (Sun Dial) — Skagaströnd, Iceland. Sculpture of stone and metal with text

1985–2015

  • Litill, Tritill, Karlsson og fuglarnir — Snælandsskóli, Kópavogur, Iceland. A Hungarian fairy tale depicted in 50 ceramic sculptures and texts on inside and outside walls

2010

  • Mótun lands (The Formation of Land) — Department of Geology and Natural Science (Askja), University of Iceland, Reykjavík, Iceland. A wall of tiling and engraving with text from Völuspá and poetry by Jónas Hallgrímsson

1995

  • Þiljur (Claddings) — University of Iceland, Department of Physiology, Reykjavík, Iceland. Three wooden panels with sculpture and engraving about the body, with text from the Edda transcribed into Wingdings (carving by Hannes Lárusson)

1985

  • Silfur Egils — Varmárskóli, Mosfellsbær, Iceland. Ceramic outdoor wall piece covered in white glaze. Story of Egill Skallagrímsson and his two slaves hiding jewellery and gold, then killing the slaves

1982

  • Jónsmessa — Búnaðarbanki (later relocated), Reykjavík, Iceland. Wall piece made in aluminium

2014

  • Sólur (Sun Dial), sculpture of stone and metal with text, Skagaströnd, Iceland

1985–2015

  • Litill, Tritill, Karlsson og fuglarnir. A Hungarian fairy tale depicted in 50 ceramic sculptures and texts on inside and outside walls of Snælandsskóli, a junior and middle school in Kópavogur, Iceland

2010

  • Mótun lands (The Formation of Land), a wall of tiling and engraving with text from Völuspá about the formation of the earth and poetry by Jónas Hallgrímsson about the mountain Skjaldbreiður, in the entrance to the Department of Geology and Natural Science (Askja), University of Iceland

1995

  • Þiljur (Claddings), three wooden panels with sculpture and engraving about the body with words depicted in the University of Iceland Department of Physiology. Text taken from the Edda and transcribed into Wingdings. Carving done by Hannes Lárusson.

1985

  • Silfur Egils, ceramic outdoor wall piece covered in white glaze on Varmárskóli in Mosfellsbær, Iceland. Tells the story of Egill Skallagrímsson and his two slaves hiding boxes of jewellery and gold, and then the killing of the slaves.

1982

  • Jónsmessa, wall piece made in aluminium originally in Búnaðarbanki (later relocated)
